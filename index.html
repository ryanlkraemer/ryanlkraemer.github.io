About Me

I'm an ice monster that can only survive in sub-freezing temperatures and all I do is ice skate.


Just kidding. I'm not an ice monster, my preferred weather is a breezy 60 degrees, and I barely know how to ice skate. I am Minnesotan, though.


As I've grown up, I've taken lots of different interests: LEGOs to engineering, video games to coding, listening to music to making music. I guess I tend to get inspired and enthralled by the things I find fascinating.


I get so enthralled, in fact, that I end up losing track of time and dedicating a lot of effort to things like constructing a wind turbine and building a website (yes, this website). You can find some of the things I'm proud of here by clicking the tabs at the top.

At School

In school, I take pride in my work and invest in my learning. STEM classes intrigue me the most; since kindergarten, I've been excited for every new topic I learn about physics, chemistry, geology, calculus, and basically anything else. The countless hours I've spent honed in on my work in the engineering lab are a testament to my passion and work ethic.


I also know how important it is to think about ideas, history, and logic. My STEM classes fascinate me the most, but that by no means degrades the importance and interest I place in the humanities. I'm proud of the analytical skills I've developed in debate and of the syntheses I've formed in essays.


My activities at school have played a major role in my life. My teams mean a lot to me, and I've found a lot of close friends in cross country and debate. I know the importance of community and collaboration in both helping one and helping all.
