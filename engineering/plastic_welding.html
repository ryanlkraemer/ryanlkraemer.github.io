Plastic Welding

My flipper partner and I needed a way to weld joints together, and we noticed that our school's shop lacked precedent for plastic welding. We ordered a designated roll of filament and I made instructions, listed below, for the whole class to have access to.


I've actually used this a lot since, and it plays an important role in my wind turbine as a coupling agent between the shaft and the generator.


Materials & Prep

  • Goggles NECESSARY
  • Breathe through your shirt or a mask
  • Turn on fan or air filter nearby
  • Dremel
  • 2.85mm printing filament (white, found in shop)
  • the targeted piece(s)

Steps

  1. Take all safety precautions above
  2. Plug in Dremel and set to 15
  3. Cut short (~1 in.) bit of 2.85mm filament and straighten as much as possible
  4. Place filament into Dremel slot with ~1/3 of strip in hole
  5. Hold pieces steady in desired final position while you work (Quick Clamps may help)
  6. The filament takes a little bit of friction to heat up; slide lightly it around the surface and joint in question
  7. Once filament starts to readily melt off, continue to slide lightly around joint/break
  8. HOLDING IN ONE SPOT OR PRESSING HARD WILL MELT THROUGH MATERIALS, ESPECIALLY 3D PRINTS! Glide lightly over surface
  9. Continue until weld is smooth, consistent, and solid
